INDIRANAGARA                               VV PURAM               KORAMANGALA



VV Puram is Bangalore’s most famous Food Street and a complete        must-do for anyone visiting or
staying in Bangalore. Till you get there and try the food, you        don’t know what you’re missing.
This guide attempts to cover almost each of the outlets starting      when one enters the street at
Sajjan Rao Circle. Before that a few basic guidelines about this      street and what we suggest
you keep in mind while planning this gastronomical adventure.
To taste maximum number of dishes and make the most of this trail,     it in a group of 8 or more
people (who do not mind sharing food). There is so much on offer       just a couple of bites of
the top items at each joint will fill you up to a point when the       may crave but the stomach
will refuse to take in any more. No point eating one or two items      heading back, we say.
VV Puram comes alive around 7:30 – 8:00 in the evening. While some     the shops start serving
from 5:30-6:00 PM onwards, all of them are ready only by 7:00 PM.
Weekends are extremely crowded so get there a little early. The        can mean having to wait
a fair bit of time at some of the shops.

Finding parking is not extremely easy on weekends – try one of the     leading into Sajjan Rao
Circle if you can’t find parking around the circle itself.
Do use dustbins to dispose of the plates etc. Most shops will have     dustbins placed at prominent
locations.
